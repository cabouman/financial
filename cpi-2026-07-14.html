What happened

Almost all of June's decline came from energy. Oil prices fell roughly 25% during the month as hostilities in the Middle East eased — though that relief may prove temporary, as the ceasefire was declared over in early July. The more durable news is core inflation: flat on the month and down to 2.6% annually, its best reading of the year.

The context matters: this spring, headline inflation surged from 2.4% in January to 4.2% in May — the sharp spike visible at the right edge of the chart. One good month, driven mostly by an oil swing, does not unwind that. The Federal Reserve is still broadly expected to raise rates in September, and Governor Waller said this week it would take several months of readings like this one to convince him inflation is returning to the 2% target.

For a portfolio built around the stock/bond/cash question (see the portfolio analysis), this is exactly the regime uncertainty that decides whether bonds hedge equities or fall with them: in inflation-shock months, stock–bond correlation rises and cash is the safer defensive asset; in growth-shock recessions, duration is the hedge. May's spike and June's plunge are a reminder of how fast that regime signal flips.
